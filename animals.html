Meet the Animals We Love

Explore some of Alaska’s most iconic animals, from land predators to marine life adapted to cold northern environments.

Caribou

Also known as reindeer, caribou are migratory animals facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.

Wolves

Wolves are apex predators that help maintain balance in ecosystems across Alaska’s wild landscapes.

Red Fox

Red foxes are highly adaptable animals known for their intelligence, speed, and striking appearance.

Brown Bear

Brown bears are among Alaska’s most powerful mammals and are often seen near rivers during salmon season.

Sitka Black-Tailed Deer

This deer species is well adapted to coastal forests and mountainous regions in southeastern Alaska.

Wolverine

Wolverines are strong, solitary animals known for surviving in harsh, snowy, and remote conditions.

Canada Lynx

The Canada lynx is a stealthy wild cat with thick fur and large paws that help it move through deep snow.

Sea Otter

Sea otters spend much of their lives in the water and play an important role in coastal marine ecosystems.

Beluga Whale

Beluga whales are social marine mammals recognized by their white color and expressive vocal sounds.

Flying Squirrel

Flying squirrels glide between trees using a skin membrane, allowing them to travel efficiently through forests.

Golden Eagle

Golden eagles are large birds of prey with excellent vision and impressive hunting ability.

Polar Bear

Polar bears live in Arctic coastal areas and depend heavily on sea ice for hunting and survival.
