Meet the Plants We Love

These plants survive in some of the coldest and toughest environments, making Alaska’s ecosystems rich and unique.

Dwarf Willow

A tiny tundra plant that stays low to the ground to survive cold temperatures and strong winds.

Mountain Avens

This flower is common in alpine and tundra regions and is known for its white petals and yellow center.

Cloudberries

Cloudberries are bright, edible berries that grow in cool northern bogs and wetlands.

Alders

Alders are hardy shrubs and trees that help improve soil quality and grow well in moist environments.

Eelgrass

Eelgrass grows underwater in coastal habitats and provides shelter and food for marine animals.

Lichen

Lichen is a partnership between fungi and algae, and it thrives in cold, rocky, and open landscapes.

Rockweed

Rockweed is a coastal seaweed that attaches to rocks and supports life in tidal ecosystems.

Cattails

Cattails are wetland plants with tall stems and seed heads, often found around lakes and marshes.

Salmonberries

These colorful berries grow on shrubs in moist forests and are important to wildlife and people.

Fireweed

Fireweed is a bright wildflower that often grows in open areas after disturbance, such as fire.

Arctic Poppy

This small flower turns toward the sun and grows in cold northern tundra environments.

Lupine

Lupine adds vibrant color to Alaskan fields and helps enrich the soil where it grows.
